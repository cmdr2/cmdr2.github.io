Bah. I just lost the entire list of test cases that I spent most of yesterday collecting and planning.

Over the past few months, I've been using a new text editor that I wrote for myself using AI. I haven't really reviewed even a single line of code for the text editor, it's worked fairly well.

Today, some weird bug caused the entire text file to get deleted.

This is a bitter lesson for trusting vibecoded stuff for things that need reliability. AI is ultimately a tool, and it'll write what I tell it to write, and won't write what I don't tell it to write. There's no point in blaming your tools.

It's tempting to feel like "I wouldn't have written such an inconsistent system", and it's probably true as well. But it was my responsibility to get AI to write test cases related to data reliability (and then for me to review them thoroughly), and I didn't do that.
